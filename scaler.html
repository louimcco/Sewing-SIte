What is Pattern Scaling?

Pattern scaling is the process of taking an existing pattern and adjusting the measurements to fit yourself/ your client. This is a super handy skill for creating pieces tailored to perfection, ultimately creating a super well-fitting look.

Instructions:

The pattern scaler is very simple to use and is intended to make the scaling of multiple measurements more convenient. The scaler doesn't work in any specific unit so feel free to use whichever unit you prefer.

First, calculate the ratio with which you will be working. On a more complex project, the ratio may change for each measurement of the garment. However, if you're just looking to size the whole pattern up then a single ratio should suffice. To find this ratio, take a measurement of one aspect of the pattern (i.e. the waist meaaurement of a front panel for a top) then divide your corresponding measurement by this value.

For example: If I have a pattern piece of with a waist measurement of 10cm, but my actual measurement for that piece should be 12cm, then the ratio I need to work with is 1.2 (12 divided by 10). Therefore, the chest measurement for the same piece can the be multiplied by 1.2 to get the appropriate size.

Some patterns will come with an accompanying scale which can be used instead. Say 3cm depicted on the pattern is actually 1cm in real-life then there's a 1:3 ratio of pattern to reality.
